IELTS Writing Task 2는 40분 동안 최소 250단어를 써야 하는 논설문(Essay)입니다. 이 주제는 과학의 목적이 '인류 삶의 개선'에 있어야 한다는 주장에 얼마나 동의하는지 묻고 있습니다.
막연하게 시작하기보다, 고득점(7.0+)을 타겟으로 하는 4단계 사고법과 실제 답안 작성 과정을 보여드릴게요.
------------------------------
## 1단계: 전략 짜기 (Planning - 5분)
무작정 쓰기 시작하면 중간에 논리가 꼬입니다. 먼저 입장을 정하세요.

* 나의 입장: 강력히 동의한다 (Strongly Agree).
* 이유 1 (의학): 질병 치료와 생명 연장 (예: 백신, 수술 기술).
* 이유 2 (편의성/기술): 일상의 효율성 증대 (예: 인터넷, 교통, 에너지).
* 반론 고려 (살짝): 순수 과학(우주 탐사 등)도 중요하지만, 결국 그것도 장기적으로는 인류를 위한 것이다.

------------------------------
## 2단계: 서론 - 주제 소개 및 입장 표명 (Introduction)
방법: 문제를 내 단어로 바꾸고(Paraphrasing), 내 의견을 분명히 밝힙니다.

작성 예시:
It is often argued that the primary objective of scientific research should be the enhancement of human well-being. I personally agree with this view because science has always been the most powerful tool for solving practical problems and advancing the quality of our lives.

------------------------------
## 3단계: 본론 - 논리 전개 (Body Paragraphs)
각 문단의 첫 문장은 주제문(Topic Sentence)으로 시작합니다.

* 본론 1 (의학적 혜택):
First of all, science plays a crucial role in medical advancements. (과학은 의학 발전에 결정적 역할을 한다.) 그 예로 과거에는 치명적이었던 전염병들이 백신과 항생제 덕분에 통제 가능해졌음을 언급합니다.
* 본론 2 (기술적 편의성):
Secondly, scientific innovations have transformed our daily routines. (과학 혁신은 일상을 바꿨다.) 스마트폰, 인터넷, 친환경 에너지 기술 등을 통해 우리가 더 빠르고 편리하게 소통하며 지속 가능한 삶을 살 수 있게 되었음을 설명합니다.

------------------------------
## 4단계: 결론 - 요약 및 재강조 (Conclusion)
새로운 내용을 쓰지 말고 위에서 한 말을 정리합니다.

작성 예시:
In conclusion, while some may argue for pure academic curiosity, I believe that the ultimate value of science lies in its practical application to human life. By focusing on improving health and convenience, science fulfills its most noble purpose.

## 💡 고득점을 위한 '치트키' 어휘 (Vocabulary)
단순한 단어 대신 이런 표현들을 섞어보세요.

| 일반적인 표현 | 고득점용 표현 |
|---|---|
| improve lives | enhance human well-being |
| most important aim | the primary objective / the ultimate goal |
| I agree | I firmly believe / I hold the view that |

## 📝 실제 시험에서 바로 써먹는 팁

   1. "To what extent"에 답하기: 그냥 동의한다(I agree) 보다는 "I completely agree" 혹은 "I agree to a large extent"처럼 정도를 표현해주면 좋습니다.
   2. 구체적인 예시 활용: '과학이 좋다'라고만 하지 말고 'mRNA 백신 개발'이나 '재생 에너지 시스템'처럼 구체적인 단어를 한두 개 던져주면 설득력이 확 올라갑니다.

이 주제에 대해 본인의 첫 번째 이유(의학이나 기술 등)를 한 문장으로 영어로 써보시겠어요? 제가 실전에서 바로 쓸 수 있는 멋진 문장으로 다듬어 드릴게요!

Topic: The most important aim of science should be to improve people’s lives. To what extent do you agree or disagree?
It is often argued that the primary objective of scientific research and innovation should be the enhancement of human well-being. I completely agree with this statement, as the ultimate value of any intellectual pursuit lies in its ability to address practical challenges and improve the quality of life for global citizens.
The most compelling reason for this view is the role of science in medical advancement. Throughout history, the greatest scientific breakthroughs have been those that eradicated diseases and extended life expectancy. For instance, the development of vaccines and antibiotics has transformed once-fatal illnesses into manageable conditions. If scientific focus were shifted away from these human-centric goals, we would lose the most effective tool we have for reducing physical suffering. Therefore, the moral and practical justification for science is rooted in its capacity to preserve and improve human health.
Furthermore, science is essential for solving the modern world's complex logistical and environmental problems. As the global population grows, we face unprecedented challenges in food security, energy consumption, and communication. Scientific innovations, such as high-yield crop varieties and renewable energy technologies like solar and wind power, are direct responses to these needs. These advancements do not merely satisfy academic curiosity; they ensure that people have access to electricity, clean water, and nutrition. In this sense, science acts as a bridge between theoretical knowledge and the survival of our species.
Some might argue that "pure science," such as space exploration or theoretical physics, should be pursued for the sake of knowledge alone. However, even these fields ultimately serve humanity. For example, satellite technology, which originated from space research, now facilitates global GPS systems and weather forecasting, both of which are vital for daily safety and commerce. Thus, even the most abstract sciences eventually find their purpose in benefiting society.
In conclusion, I firmly believe that the fundamental goal of science must remain the improvement of human lives. Whether through life-saving medicine or sustainable technology, the success of scientific endeavors should be measured by the positive impact they have on the world.
