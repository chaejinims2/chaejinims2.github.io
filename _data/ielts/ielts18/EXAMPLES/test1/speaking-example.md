각 파트별로 시험장에서 바로 쓸 수 있는 '고득점 답변 가이드'를 만들어 드릴게요.

## 🎤 Part 1: Paying bills (가벼운 문답)
팁: 너무 짧게 대답하지 말고, '이유'나 '추가 정보'를 한 문장씩 더 붙이세요.

   1. What kinds of bills do you have to pay?
   * "Well, like most people, I have to settle several monthly utilities, such as electricity, water, and gas. On top of that, I also pay for my mobile phone plan and high-speed internet subscription."
   2. How do you usually pay your bills - in cash or by another method? [Why?]
   * "I hardly ever use cash these days. I prefer online banking or mobile apps because they are incredibly convenient. Most of my bills are set to automatic transfer, so I don't have to worry about them every month."
   3. Have you ever forgotten to pay a bill? [Why/Why not?]
   * "To be honest, yes, I have. A few years ago, I missed a credit card payment because I was swamped with work and simply forgot the due date. Since then, I’ve switched everything to auto-pay to avoid any late fees."
   4. Is there anything you could do to make your bills cheaper? [Why/Why not?]
   * "Definitely. I could try to conserve energy by turning off lights when I'm not in the room or using the air conditioner less during summer. Also, I should probably cancel some streaming services that I rarely watch."

## 🎤 Part 2: Describe some food or drink you learned to prepare (1~2분 스피치)
팁: 육하원칙에 따라 이야기를 풀어나가세요.
[샘플 답변: 알리오 올리오 파스타]

* Intro: "I’d like to talk about a dish called Aglio e Olio, which is a simple Italian pasta made with garlic and oil."
* When/Where: "I learned to make this about two years ago, during the COVID-19 lockdown. Since I was spending most of my time at home, I decided to improve my cooking skills."
* How: "I actually learned it through a YouTube tutorial by a famous chef. The process was quite straightforward: you just need to sauté sliced garlic in olive oil until it's golden brown, then toss in the boiled spaghetti with some red pepper flakes."
* Feeling: "Initially, I felt a bit nervous about burning the garlic, but when I finally tasted it, I felt immensely proud. It was much more delicious than I expected, and it gave me a sense of accomplishment to cook a restaurant-quality meal by myself."

## 🎤 Part 3: Discussion topics (심층 토론)
팁: 자신의 의견을 논리적으로 확장하세요. (Opinion + Reason + Example)
## [Young people and cooking]

   1. What kinds of things can children learn to cook?
   * "I think children should start with basic, low-risk tasks. For example, making a simple sandwich, boiling an egg, or preparing a bowl of cereal. As they get older, they can move on to using the stove under supervision."
   2. Do you think it is important for children to learn to cook?
   * "Absolutely. Cooking is an essential life skill. It not only teaches them about nutrition and healthy eating habits but also fosters independence. If they know how to cook, they won't have to rely on unhealthy fast food later in life."
   
## [Working as a chef]

   1. How enjoyable do you think it would be to work as a professional chef?
   * "While it might be rewarding to see people enjoying your food, I imagine it's also incredibly stressful. Chefs have to work long hours in a high-pressure environment, often standing all day in a hot kitchen. It requires a lot of passion and physical stamina."
   2. How much influence do celebrity chefs have?
   * "I believe they have a profound influence. Through TV shows and social media, they can make certain ingredients or healthy diets go viral. For instance, many people started trying 'home-cooking' during the pandemic because of the easy-to-follow recipes shared by celebrity chefs."
