
The graph below gives information about the percentage of the population in four Asian countries living in cities from 1970 to 2020, with predictions for 2030 and 2040.

Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

Write at least 150 words.

------------------------------
## 1. Introduction
목표: 문제의 문장을 내 말투로 바꾸기 (Paraphrasing)
사고: 
	1. '그래프'는 line graph
	2. '보여준다'는 compares로
	3. '도시 인구 비율'은 proportions of people living in cities로 바꾸자
	4. 기간도 명시하자.

The line graph compares the proportions of people living in cities in the Philippines, Malaysia, Thailand and Indonesia from 1970 to 2020, with projections for 2030 and 2040.

------------------------------
## 2. Overall
목표: 세부 수치 없이 '가장 큰 그림' 2~3개만 말하기
사고: 
	1. 전체적으로 다 오르네? (All increase)
	2. 누가 제일 높아? (Malaysia)
	3. 특이한 애는? (필리핀은 중간에 꺾이고, 인도네시아는 확 올라오네.)

Overall, urbanisation increases in all four countries. Malaysia shows the most consistent and highest growth, while Thailand remains the least urbanised up to 2020. The Philippines is the only country to experience a mid-period decline, whereas Indonesia rises sharply after 2000 and overtakes the Philippines from around 2010.

------------------------------
## 3. Body 1 - 시작점과 선두 그룹
목표: 1970년 수치 비교 + 가장 깔끔한 데이터(말레이시아) 먼저 정리
사고: 1970년 당시 1위는 필리핀(32%쯤), 2위 말레이시아(30%). 태국(18%)과 인도네시아(13%)는 낮았음. 그 후 말레이시아는 계속 올라서 2040년엔 80% 넘겠네.

In 1970, the Philippines had the largest urban share at just over 30%, followed by Malaysia at about 30%, while Thailand and Indonesia were much lower, at roughly 18% and around 13% respectively. Malaysia then climbed steadily, reaching approximately 60% by 2000 and about three-quarters in 2020, and it is forecast to exceed 80% by 2040.

------------------------------
## 4. Body 2 - 변화가 많은 그룹 비교
목표: 필리핀의 하락세, 인도네시아의 역전, 태국의 완만한 상승 정리
사고: 
	1. 필리핀은 90년에 피크(50% 직전) 찍고 2010년에 43%로 떨어졌다가 다시 회복 중.
	2. 인도네시아는 2000년(30%)부터 갑자기 수직 상승해서 2040년엔 60% 넘을 듯(2/3 지점).
	3. 태국은 계속 꼴찌다가 마지막에 좀 힘을 내서 50% 정도 가겠군.

The Philippines increased to just under 50% in 1990, before falling to around 43% in 2010. It then recovers gradually, rising to the mid-40s in 2020 and is predicted to reach the mid-50s by 2040. Indonesia grows from the mid-teens in 1970 to about 30% in 2000, then jumps to roughly 43% in 2010 and is projected to approach two-thirds by 2040. Thailand increases more slowly, from under 20% to the low 30s by 2020, with a stronger rise expected to around 50% in 2040.
