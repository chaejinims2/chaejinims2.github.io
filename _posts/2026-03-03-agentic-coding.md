# What is Agentic Coding
Agentic coding uses autonomous AI agents to plan, write, test, and refine code, acting as a collaborative partner rather than just an autocomplete tool. Powered by Large Language Models (LLMs), these agents operate in a "reason and act" loop, managing entire development tasks—such as debugging or feature implementation—with minimal human intervention.

## Key Aspects of Agentic Coding
- Autonomous AI agents
- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- "Reason and act" loop
- Minimal human intervention

## Benefits and Use Cases
- Increased Productivity: Speeds up development cycles by handling boilerplate and complex, multi-step tasks.
- Improved Quality: Automated testing and continuous refinement lead to better code quality and fewer bugs.
- Focus on High-Value Tasks: Developers are freed from repetitive coding to focus on architecture and design.
